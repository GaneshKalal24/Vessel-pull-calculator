Rigging Notes

    [cite_start]
  • The MBF check applies to a single-leg wire rope sling complying with AS 1666.1[cite: 4].
    • [cite_start]
  • Pull must be conducted under steady, pre-tensioned conditions with no snatching or shock loading[cite: 4].
    • [cite_start]
  • If the element does not move as expected, the pull shall cease and the method be reassessed[cite: 4].

Assumptions

    [cite_start]
  • The vessel will rotate about the defined rotation point[cite: 4].
    • [cite_start]
  • The vessel centre of gravity is approximately coincident with the geometric centre of the base[cite: 4, 5].
    • [cite_start]
  • The vessel is stabilised by self weight alone, no allowance is made for grout adhesion, shear keys, bolts or other restraining forces[cite: 6].
    • [cite_start]
  • The vessel is sufficiently strong to support it's self weight during rotation and collapse[cite: 6].
    • [cite_start]
  • Pulling is assumed to be conducted as a steady, pre-tensioned pull with no snatching or shock loading[cite: 7].
  • Wind loading has not been considered. [cite_start]It is assumed all work will occur in light winds suitable for operation of Elevated Work Platforms[cite: 7].
    • [cite_start]
  • The vessel is mounted on a rigid base which is capable of withstanding the forces imposed due to rotation[cite: 7].
